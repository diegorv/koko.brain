# Recognizing and Preventing Team Exhaustion

## Warning signs to watch for
- Increased cynicism or negativity in standups
- Drop in code review quality — rubber-stamping approvals
- People working late but delivering less
- Skipping social events or team lunches
- Taking fewer risks, avoiding challenging tasks

## Strategies that work
1. **Protect focus time**: Block 4-hour windows where nobody can schedule meetings
2. **Say no to scope creep**: If we add something, we remove something else
3. **Celebrate small wins**: Not just ship dates — also refactors, mentoring, good docs
4. **Rotate on-call fairly**: Nobody should be on-call more than once per month
5. **Encourage actual vacations**: Time off where you truly disconnect, no Slack checking

## Recovery actions
When someone is already showing signs of exhaustion:
- Reduce their workload by 30% for 2-3 sprints
- Pair them with a junior for mentoring (changes pace, builds connection)
- Offer flexible hours or remote days
- Have an honest conversation — acknowledge the situation without judgment
