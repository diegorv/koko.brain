# Garden Journal — January 2025

## Current plants

### Balcony herbs
- **Basil**: Growing well, harvested twice this month. Getting leggy — needs pruning.
- **Rosemary**: Thriving. Barely needs watering. Used in the roast chicken last week.
- **Mint**: Invasive as expected. Moved to its own pot to prevent takeover.
- **Cilantro**: Bolted in the heat. Need to replant from seed next month.

### Indoor plants
- **Monstera deliciosa**: New leaf unfurling! Fourth leaf since repotting in October.
- **Pothos**: Propagated 3 cuttings in water. Roots visible after 2 weeks.
- **Snake plant**: Completely maintenance-free. Haven't watered in 3 weeks.
- **Fiddle leaf fig**: Lost two lower leaves. Might be overwatering — switching to bi-weekly.

## Tasks for February
- [ ] Start tomato seedlings indoors (6-8 weeks before last frost)
- [ ] Repot the monstera — roots coming out of drainage holes
- [ ] Order fertilizer for spring growing season
- [ ] Build a simple drip irrigation system for the balcony herbs

## Lessons learned
- Terracotta pots dry out much faster than plastic in summer
- The south-facing balcony gets too hot for lettuce — need shade cloth
- Companion planting basil near tomatoes really does help with pests
