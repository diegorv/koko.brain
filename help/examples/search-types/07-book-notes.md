# Book Notes: Deep Work by Cal Newport

## Core thesis
The ability to perform deep work is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable. Those who cultivate this skill will thrive.

## Key concepts

### Deep Work vs Shallow Work
- **Deep work**: Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit
- **Shallow work**: Non-cognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks, often performed while distracted

### The 4 Rules
1. **Work deeply**: Create rituals and routines that minimize willpower needed to start deep work
2. **Embrace boredom**: Train your brain to resist distraction even outside of work
3. **Quit social media**: Apply the craftsman approach — adopt tools only if benefits substantially outweigh costs
4. **Drain the shallows**: Reduce shallow obligations ruthlessly

## Favorite quotes
> "If you don't produce, you won't thrive — no matter how skilled or talented you are."

> "Two core abilities for thriving in the new economy: the ability to quickly master hard things, and the ability to produce at an elite level."

## How I'm applying this
- Blocked 9am-12pm every day for deep work (no Slack, no email)
- Moved all meetings to afternoon blocks
- Deleted Twitter and Instagram from my phone
- Using a paper notebook for planning instead of digital tools during focus blocks
